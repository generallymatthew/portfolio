On Device Activation
for Republic Wireless

The hardest part of designing this app was figuring out the app's information architecture. The Relay app must work as all 3 things:
Manage devices & channelsA location tracker
A communication tool

We wanted the experience of communication part to act and look like the relay stand alone device
Welcome Screen
User testing taught us that users did not know if they had an account or what email they used when signing up.

To solve this problem we just gave a single form field to fill out. On the back end we would query all know accounts to see if there was an existing account with that email if so then the phone would activate on that account . If not we treated it like a new customer account
Number Selection
During testing, we framed this question in a way that confused our customers, leading to split accounts.
So we tried a different way of asking the same question.

Once we reframed the question by asking them about which phone number they wanted on this new phone the confusion was alleviated, saving us many hours of support time from no longer having to process hundreds of split accounts manually.
Plan Selection
We thought initially that users would be interested in seeing the unlimited wifi calling and texting, instead it just confused them. They expect it to be unlimited.

The one thing that they did care about what how much cell data came with the plan, they also cared what type of cell data they were getting. It was important to be clear that the cell data was high speed. Our contract with Sprint did not allow us to reference their network in anyway
Order Confirmation
Due to 'Truth in Taxation' legislation passed, we had to show a detailed breakdown of all taxes applied to any order, but we could not calculate taxes until we knew what billing zip code they would use. Hence we needed a 'confirm order'.

Our users wanted to be able to view the details, but did not need them to be expanded by default.
Activation Complete
This workflow was successful and was mentioned in many of the product reviews, for ease of use. Our open ticket rates were less than we has expected.
On Device Activation
for Republic Wireless

With no stores for customers to walk into, we had to have away for our users to be able to activate their own phones once they arrived in the mail.
Home Screen
How do we create a home screen that allowed the user to do any of the core 3 things
  • We separated everything to do with the devices into the side navigation drawer including tracking
  • We created a bottom app bar to be where you interact with channels
  • Lastly we created a simple interface for communicating, mirroring the industrial design and interaction pattern on the physical device
Channel Store
We needed a mobile store to allow people to discover the new channels that we were adding every month, as well as understand how the channel works and how to set it up on the Relay devices

Channel Store

Anywhere Mobile
Due to a technical limitation of the way android secures their internal databases for sms & mms, we could only access these messages if we asked for specific permission during installation of our own app, therefore we had to create a mobile app of our own.

Messaging apps are deceptively complex from a technical standpoint, so we choose to fork the native Messenger app and and re-skinned it with our brand and other design patterns from the desktop app.
