# Site Goals & Outcomes

## Primary Goal
Land a UX Strategist or Senior UX Designer role by September 2026. The site should make any visitor — recruiter or hiring manager — walk away believing Matthew thinks strategically, not just visually, and would be someone they'd genuinely want to work with.

---

## Target Role
- **Title:** UX Strategist or Senior UX Designer
- **Industry:** Open — culture, role quality, and team matter more than sector
- **Differentiator to communicate:** Strategic thinking + AI product design expertise + strong personality that comes through in the work

---

## Site Goals

| # | Goal | How the site addresses it | How to measure |
|---|------|--------------------------|----------------|
| 1 | **Establish credibility** | Case studies show real product decisions, not just visual polish. Essays demonstrate thought leadership on AI + UX. | Time on page, depth of scroll on case study pages |
| 2 | **Communicate AI expertise** | Four of eight case studies are AI-focused (Blackbaud). Writing section covers AI trust, ethics, and empathy. | Do recruiters/hiring managers mention AI expertise in outreach? |
| 3 | **Show strategic thinking** | Go/no-go decisions, research-led design, cross-functional collaboration stories. Not just "I made it look good." | Do interviewers reference the decision-making stories specifically? |
| 4 | **Make it easy to take action** | Resume in nav, email in hero, LinkedIn prominent. No hunting required. | Click-through rate on Resume, email, and LinkedIn links |
| 5 | **Feel like someone worth working with** | Voice in the writing section. Personality in copy. A site that feels designed, not assembled. | Does the site itself reflect the quality claimed in the work? |

---

## Success Outcomes

### Short-term (by August 2026)
- [ ] Site is live and indexed
- [ ] At least 3 inbound recruiter or hiring manager contacts referencing specific case studies
- [ ] Resume downloaded or requested at least once per week
- [ ] No critical mobile usability issues

### Medium-term (by September 2026)
- [ ] At least 2 first-round interviews secured where the portfolio was a factor
- [ ] At least one hiring manager references the writing section or a specific case study decision unprompted
- [ ] Job offer received

### Long-term (after the job)
- [ ] Site continues as a living document — new work added as it happens
- [ ] Writing section updated with new thinking as AI + UX evolves
- [ ] Used as a reference in interviews at the next role too

---

## Primary Visitors

Both recruiters and hiring managers are expected in roughly equal numbers.

**Recruiter needs:** Recognizable company names, clear job title, fast path to resume. Decision window: ~10 seconds on the homepage.

**Hiring manager needs:** Evidence of strategic thinking, real outcomes, cross-functional credibility. Will read at least one case study and possibly an essay.

**Design:** The homepage has to work for both. The case studies have to work especially well for hiring managers.

---

## Open Questions to Track

- [ ] Are visitors finding the writing section, or is it invisible below the case studies?
- [ ] Which case studies get the most time-on-page? (Signals what's resonating)
- [ ] Are visitors on mobile bouncing before reading anything?
- [ ] Is "UX Strategist" landing — or do people not know that title and assume a narrower role?
- [ ] Is the AI expertise coming through clearly enough, or does it feel like a supporting detail rather than a headline?
