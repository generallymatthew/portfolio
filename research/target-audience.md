# Target Audience

## Primary Visitors

### 1. Technical Recruiter
**Goal:** Quickly assess fit for an open UX/Product Design role.  
**Behaviour:** Lands on homepage, scans the hero, clicks a case study, skims for recognisable company names and outcomes. Probably on desktop but may be on mobile.  
**What they need from the site:** Clear job title, credible company names, fast path to resume.  
**Risk:** Bounces if the value proposition isn't clear within 10 seconds.

### 2. Hiring Manager / Design Lead
**Goal:** Evaluate depth of thinking, process maturity, and cultural fit.  
**Behaviour:** Reads case studies more carefully, looks for evidence of decision-making and cross-functional collaboration, may read one or two essays.  
**What they need from the site:** Process detail, outcomes with context, a sense of voice and point of view.  
**Risk:** Loses confidence if case studies feel thin or if the writing contradicts the work shown.

### 3. Peer Designer / Design Community
**Goal:** Read the writing, get inspired, or vet a potential collaborator.  
**Behaviour:** Often lands directly on an essay from a share or link. May or may not visit the homepage.  
**What they need from the site:** Substantive, well-argued writing. Easy path to the rest of the site if interested.  
**Risk:** Feels the writing is too safe or generic.

---

## Secondary Visitors
- Former colleagues checking in on current work
- Potential freelance clients (not the primary audience but should not be alienated)

---

## What Visitors Are NOT
- Students looking for inspiration templates (the site should not feel like a tutorial)
- General consumers (no need for e-commerce or onboarding flows)
