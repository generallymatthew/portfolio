The Danger of the Infinite "Yes-Man"

We've trained AI to be polite. In fact, we've trained it so well that its default setting is often hyper-agreeable. If you present a half-baked theory or a deeply flawed idea, the typical AI response is still an enthusiastic, "That's a fantastic point!"

While this instant validation feels great initially, it's a trap. When an AI is too agreeable, several things break down:

  • The Echo Chamber Effect: If the AI only mirrors your perspective, it stops generating genuine value. You aren't getting a collaborator; you're getting a sounding board that just nods along.
  • Decoupling from Reality: True innovation and solid problem-solving require friction. Without pushback, bad ideas survive longer than they should because no one, or nothing, is checking the math.
  • Loss of Trust: If an assistant agrees with everything you say, its praise becomes meaningless. You quickly learn that its validation is a programmatic script, not an earned assessment.
The Trade-off: Better Outcomes, Less "Fun"

If you intentionally temper your AI's agreeability (instructing it to critique your logic, point out blind spots, and challenge your assumptions), a fascinating psychological shift happens: the tool becomes significantly less enjoyable to use.

It turns a breezy, effortless interaction into an intellectual workout. It forces you to defend your positions, re-evaluate your data, and sit with the discomfort of being wrong.

But that drop in immediate user enjoyment is exactly where the value lies.

"True delight shouldn't come from a frictionless conversation; it should come from a superior outcome."

On designing agentic AI
Designing for Outcomes Over Comfort

In product design, we often aim for "delight." But when it comes to partnering with agentic AI, true delight shouldn't come from a frictionless conversation; it should come from a superior outcome.

By introducing intentional, constructive friction into your AI interactions, you trade short-term validation for long-term clarity. It might not feel like a warm hug, but it's how you get to the truth.
